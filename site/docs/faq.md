# FAQ

## Setup

### How long does setup take?
About five to ten minutes for the first install: connect a brain, pair WhatsApp, confirm safe permissions, and send the first command.

### Is this a WhatsApp bot for replying to customers?
No — not by default. Wira is a private agent surface for the owner/operator. It lives on your computer and you talk to it from your phone. Customer-facing workflows are a separate later extension, not the main product thesis.

### Do I need to install anything?
Yes. Wira Local is a local product. The point is that the real agent lives on your machine, not on somebody else's server.

## Agent behavior

### What does Vera actually do?
Vera is your branded agent surface. Under the hood, the goal is a real Hermes runtime: tools, skills, memory, and local access you approve. You should be able to ask for real work, not just drafted text.

### Why not just use ChatGPT?
Because ChatGPT is still mostly a chat surface. Wira is the bridge into agentic work on your own machine: files, browser, terminal, memory, and growing capabilities — all reachable from your phone.

### Can I rename Vera?
Yes. Vera is the default local name because it's friendlier than dropping raw agent terminology on day one, but the product should let you rename the agent after setup.

### Will I be able to use Hermes directly later?
Yes — that's part of the goal. Wira should make Hermes Desktop and CLI discoverable organically once you're ready, instead of forcing you to learn the whole stack before you get value.

## Privacy and safety

### Does Wira run locally?
That is the intended Local thesis: your agent lives on your computer, with local state and local permissions. WhatsApp is the control surface, not the main runtime.

### What can the agent access?
Only what you allow. The long-term safe model is explicit owner lock plus deliberate permissions for files, folders, browser, terminal, and automations.

### Can other people talk to my agent?
Not by default. Local Wira should be owner-locked so your private operator surface doesn't become a public bot by accident.

## WhatsApp

### Why WhatsApp at all?
Because it's already where people live. Wira removes the friction of learning a new interface first. You start from your phone, then grow into richer desktop and CLI use later.

### Does it reply in group chats?
That is not the primary product thesis. The important path is owner-to-agent control, not public group-chat automation.

### What about voice notes and images?
Those are reasonable later capabilities, but they are secondary to the core promise: a real local agent you can command from your phone.

## Billing

### When am I charged?
On signup, then monthly or yearly depending on the plan.

### What am I actually paying for?
You're paying for a branded, lower-friction way into owning a real agent on your own machine — with setup, pairing, packaging, and a clean path into deeper Hermes use.

## Limits and roadmap

### What's on the roadmap?
- Vera-first local onboarding
- owner lock + permission controls
- real Hermes runtime bridge
- discoverable Desktop/CLI handoff
- optional business workflow packs
- only later, if still wanted: responder/customer-facing extensions
